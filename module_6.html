RMPP_PCOM7E

The research methods and professional pratice module exposes the cyber-security student to a methodology for critically assessing wider social issues that afffect computing professionals. Additionally, students are required to develop a research proposal and participate in many written assignments, presumably to develop more effective communication skills needed to interact with those outside technical diciplines.

Module learning outcomes

Learning outcomes stated at the outset of the module, (listed below), were not specifically traceable to the assignment criteria as a one for one match, rather the intention was to interprete assignments and provide a point of view, which may require the e-portfolio reader to be somewhat familiar with computer science and cyber security matters.

  • Understand and perform research based on the scientific method
  • Understand research strategies and design a research project
  • Perform research activites to develop competence while critically assessing both the research data and the student's own project design

Collaborative learning discussion one

Students were asked to review a numer of case studies pertaining to the ACM code of ethics. I chose the employment harassment case study since it is something I have been involved in previous employment as a digitial forensic investigator. Unfortunately I was unable to find any academic or legal evidence that such a code will serve as a reasonable deterent to someone who choses not follow the code.

Literature Review Outline

Class participants were encouraged to submit a draft of the litereature review for formative feedback. as some students find the format unfamiliar and formative feedback support correction prior to summative submission, often resulting in a better mark.
Two feed back items stood out, "avoid the introduction of new ideas in the conclusion section" and "do not segment with multiple headings and sub-sections as the report is quiet short." This direction was counter to numerous criticisms received from previous Essex instructors, requesting more "markers" aid reader navigation despite those reports being shorter.

Formative feedback was incorporated into the final submission along with almost 50% more research into the subject, finding the best sources to tie the five identified areas of review into a more cohesive narrative. A review of the draft and final submission will show this extensive rework.

Collaborative learning discussion two

Discussion two was further exploration of ethics, this case study focused on the presentation of privately funded research. Similar to discussion one, my inital position was ethics are a personal matter, and my advice to the researcher would be to present a report that would stand up to scientific peer scrutiny and clearly identify the private funding source. The advise given knowing individual scientists often do not experience negative consequences for questionable research and the company would likely spin the results to their own benefit or bury the study.

Both respondents to my post echoed concerns with privately funded research and discomfort with the lack of ability to prevent skewed research from becomging widely publicized. My summary post is an evolution of the ethics problem versus a postion change. Further research identified questionable research practices are quite common and may well be unknowingly committed. Our job as cyber security professionals should include maintaining a skeptical view of commercial solutions claimed to be backed by scinetific research. Academia can not provide guardrails on cyber security research, it would be naive to presume independant researchers or limited government funding could compete with the research and development levels of billion dollar security companies. Therefore, those responsible for implementing solutions must validate solution effectiveness by pushing vendors to demonstrate the value in the organization's environement or track success so the right decision is made at product renewal time.

Statistical Analysis

Statistical analysis is often key to the research process, consequently a business statistics textbook was assigned reading and two sets of workbook based assignments were provided to give students practical experience using spreadsheet functions to generate statistical artefacts such as standard deviation, and two tailed analysis.
The professor stated up front the module is not a comprehensive statistics class, which became a factor in the hands-on assigment. Running the formulas was straightforward, but I had no confidence in my interpretation what-so-ever, despite attempting the recomended reading and reviewing several additional statistics tutorials online.

Although there is technically tutor support available, this isn't the module's focus and it typically takes several days to work through foundational understanding via email so this wasn't pursued.

Capstone Project Proposal

The project proposal provides a means for stakeholders to assess the viability and value of a project. Although this is an academic setting, it closely mirrors the regularly undertaken industry process of determining the merits of a project before allocating resources.
Success criteria for academic projects, I.E., a level of contribution to existing knowledge, differ from commercial projects which are almost soley focused on improving profitability, knowledge advancement is seldom relevant unless the knowledge itself can be turned into a competitive advantage.
